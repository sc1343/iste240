Museum

New York City, one of the place that with most museums in the world

This pages provided some best Museums in NYC

NYC Transit Museum

Address: 99 Schermerhorn St
Brooklyn, NY 11201
Website: https://www.nytransitmuseum.org/
Tickets: $5 - 10
The New York Transit Museum (also called the NYC Transit Museum) is a museum that displays historical artifacts of the New York City Subway, bus, and commuter rail systems in the greater New York City metropolitan region.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art

Address: 1000 5th Ave, New York City
NY 10028-0198
Website: https://www.metmuseum.org
Tickets: $12 - 25
At New York City's most visited museum and attraction, you will experience over many different of art from around the world. Opened in 1880 and situated on Central Park, this iconic New York institution contains 5,000 years of art from prehistory to the latest in contemporary works under one roof.

American Museum of Natural History

Address: 200 Central Park West
New York, NY 10024
Website: https://www.amnh.org/
Tickets: $12 - 23
The legendary museum's motto is "To discover, interpret, and disseminate, through scientific research and education knowledge about human cultures, the natural world, and the universe." It's one of the very best museums for kids in NYC

Museum of Modern Art

Address: 11 W 53rd St, New York
NY 10019
Website: https://www.moma.org/
Tickets: $14 - 25
Founded in 1929 as the first museum dedicated entirely to contemporary art, MoMA, in Midtown East, is host to an impressive collection of modern artistic efforts in a variety of media that includes paintings, sculpture, design, and film. Some of the museum's most famous works include Vincent Van Gogh's "Starry Night" The museum offers special events and activities for families with children as young as 4, making it a very family-friendly choice.

Brooklyn Museum

Address: 200 Eastern Pkwy
Brooklyn, NY 11238
Website: https://www.brooklynmuseum.org/
Tickets: $10 - 16
The Brooklyn Museum is an art museum located in the New York City borough of Brooklyn. The museum is New York City's third largest in physical size and holds an art collection with roughly 1.5 million works. It was planned to be the largest art museum in the world. The museum initially struggled to maintain its building and collection, only to be revitalized in the late 20th century, thanks to major renovations. Significant areas of the collection include antiquities, specifically their collection of Egyptian antiquities spanning over 3,000 years.
